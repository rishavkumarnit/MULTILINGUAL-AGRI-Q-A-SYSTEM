# Maize Irrigation and Fertilization

Maize yields are unusually sensitive to short water or nitrogen gaps around flowering —
more so than wheat or rice — because pollination happens in a narrow window and cannot
be recovered later in the season.

## Critical irrigation stages

1. **Knee-high stage (30-35 days after sowing)** — supports early root and stalk
   development; moderate stress here is tolerable.
2. **Tasseling and silking (55-65 days after sowing)** — the single most critical
   stage. Even 2-3 days of water stress during silking can cause poor grain set from
   failed pollination, visible later as bare patches or thin cobs.
3. **Grain filling (75-95 days after sowing)** — determines kernel weight; stress here
   produces shrunken kernels even on otherwise well-pollinated cobs.

## Nitrogen management

- Split nitrogen application into three doses rather than applying it all at sowing:
  roughly 25% at sowing, 50% at knee-high stage, 25% at tasseling — this matches uptake
  to the crop's actual demand curve and reduces leaching loss.
- Nitrogen deficiency shows first as a yellow, inverted-V pattern on older (lower)
  leaves, starting from the tip along the midrib — distinct from potassium deficiency,
  which shows yellowing/browning starting at the leaf margins instead.
- Applying nitrogen too close to a heavy rain event wastes a large share of it to
  runoff; apply at least 2-3 days before or after, not immediately before, if rain is
  forecast.

## Practical guidance

- Maize does not tolerate waterlogging well — even 24-48 hours of standing water during
  early vegetative growth can significantly stunt root development.
- On sandy soils, irrigate more frequently with smaller amounts; on loam or clay,
  fewer, deeper irrigations are more efficient and encourage deeper rooting.
- Zinc deficiency is common in maize on alkaline soils and looks like broad white-yellow
  bands next to the midrib on young leaves — easily mistaken for a nitrogen problem;
  correct with zinc sulfate rather than more nitrogen if this pattern appears.
