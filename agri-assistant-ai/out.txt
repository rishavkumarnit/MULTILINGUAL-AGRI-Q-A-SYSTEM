Monitor plants from about 30 days after sowing and inspect shoot tips frequently between 40–70 days. If more than ~50% of plants have heavy shoot-tip aphid coverage, consider control but try to conserve natural enemies (ladybirds, lacewings, parasitoids) first. If you must spray, target contact insecticides or suitable systemic products at the shoot tips for best effect, avoid broad‑spectrum insecticides that kill beneficials, and do treatments outside pollinator activity times (early morning or late evening). Rotate chemistries to reduce resistance and follow label rates, preharvest intervals, and local IPM guidance.
